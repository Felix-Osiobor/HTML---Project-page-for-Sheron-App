SHERON the App

August 2018

Music artist with the passion to stay even closer to her audience decided to have a platform where she can have premium content published for her fans. Although there are many platforms for this kind of demand, however, the artist insisted that she rather have a unique platform of her own. This is where hard work and fun begins. An ios lightweight app will be developed and I am responsible for the UX and UI phase of the project.

READ MORE

THE CHALLENGE

The app has to have familiar navigation (no one wants anything that requires a manual to use), and it needs to look unique in its own way. Plus, the platform should offer an intuitive way to get connected to any new piece of media that the artist releases since this is the main reason for the app.

The app needs to have a consistent approach and connection to the artist, as well as have very visible similarity to the artist's personal style so that anyone who is current with popular media will know who this app is developed to represent. All visual cues must be in line with the artist's constant choice of colour.

DISCOVERY

Haven look at some celebrity app, I discovered that each one had a different approach. For example, one music app is owned by many celebrities at once, so the app offers music and news about all these celebrities at once. Another example, the celebrity app was focused on script writing (which is not related to our project). Also, another app was a gaming app while another was a fashion app. So far only the first app offered music and so I decided to investigate the app and learn what it offers.

Upon reviewing the app, I realised that the app seeks to offer large contents of music audios and videos, and other exclusive contents. However, the app for this project I am working on is aimed to offer exclusive music audios and videos which have not yet been released, they have about two weeks to remain with the record label before they are released to any media house. And the app is also going to offer devotionals.

So with these requirements in mind, and learning from the example mentioned before, I had to come up with user experience and user interface that really served this purpose.

APPROACH

Since the closest example has a lot of music with a wide genre, so the information architecture presentation is diffrent as content is arranged with a grid system, but in this project, I will need to lead the users straight to the music content from one artist, so there will be no need to sort artists, genre, year, etc.

WIREFRAMES

After brainstorming, I came up with sketches which I had to review many times and then I made a working wireframe solution which I still had to test by getting volunteer user involved.
